# QA-035 — validate_ports accepts reversed port ranges (lo>hi, e.g. 443-22), which nmap rejects as a fatal error

- **group**: R3   **disposition**: FIX   **severity**: medium   **dimension**: scan-procedure
- **dedup_key**: `validate-ports-accepts-reversed-range`
- **location**: scanner/scanops_scanner.py:249-272 (validate_ports; range body check ~:268-271)

## Evidence
`validate_ports` checks PORT_BODY_RE and per-digit 1..65535 but never range ordering. Verified: `validate_ports('443-22')=='443-22'`, `validate_ports('T:1000-1')` and `'100-50,22'` all pass. A reversed range flows into `nmap -p` (both single and auto paths), and nmap treats a backwards range as FATAL, so the stage exits non-zero, writes no/empty XML, and the gating ladder skips identify — a single transposition typo yields an empty failed scan. Inconsistent with `expand_targets:321` which already rejects reversed IP ranges (`lo>hi`).

## Proposed fix
In `validate_ports`, after a two-endpoint `a-b` body matches, parse both and raise ValueError when `lo > hi` (mirroring expand_targets). Open ranges (`1-`, `-1024`) have one endpoint and are unaffected.

## Notes
Not covered by QA-014 (structural malformed specs; a well-formed reversed range passes all QA-014 checks) or QA-019 (IP ranges). Verified by execution.
